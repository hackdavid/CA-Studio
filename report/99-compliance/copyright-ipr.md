# Copyright & Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)

## Public Domain Consideration

Your report should be written considering that it will be within the **public domain**.

## Ownership

Normally, you retain:

- **Copyright** over your written work.
- **Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)** over any technical work.

## Exceptions

There are rare situations where this might not be as simple, for example when:

- Working with a **company** or external client.
- Working on a **larger university project**.

## Strategies

If you need to protect your work, consider the following strategies:

| Strategy | Description |
|---|---|
| Restrict access | Inform your supervisory team that the project **cannot be made available** to other students undertaking an MSc Project. |
| Shortened report | Provide a shortened report for sharing that does **not contain the protected information**. |
| Assign IPR | Assign IPR to the external collaborator. **Take great care** when doing so — it is best to speak to your supervisor. |
| Non-disclosure agreements | Make NDAs between the external collaborator and the supervision team. |

## Recommendation

If in doubt, **speak to your supervisor** before making any IPR decisions.
