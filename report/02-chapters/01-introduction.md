## Problem Description, Context and Motivation

Your research question or problem statement should address:

- **What** is the problem you are addressing?
- **Who** is affected by the problem?
- **Where and/or when** does the problem occur?
- **Why** is it important to solve the problem?

## Objectives

Objectives are the **tangible steps** that will be taken to achieve the aims — i.e., the tasks that will be done.

## Methodology

Answer: **How are you going to undertake the project?**

Describe **HOW** you will reach your objectives, including:

- Tools
- Design methods
- Data gathering methods
- Algorithms

Tell us **WHY** you have chosen these methods in favour of others, with reference to the findings of your literature and technology review.

### Required Subheadings

- **Design**
- **Testing and Evaluation**
- **Project Management** (Gantt chart, sprints, agile methodology, Kanban board, etc.)
- **Technologies and Processes**

## Legal, Social, Ethical and Professional Considerations

All projects have legal, social, ethical and professional considerations. You are marked on your ability to identify them and take necessary actions.

- Discuss with your supervisor if unsure.
- If your project required ethical clearance, state this clearly, including how ethical considerations were managed.

## Background

Provide the reader with the **relevant contextual information** necessary to understand why your work is important.

- Reference literature and/or personal motivation here.
- Describe the context of your work (e.g., relevant to business, medicine, education, etc.).
- Explain how it addresses known issues in that context.
- This section is expected to have **at least some references** to external sources.
