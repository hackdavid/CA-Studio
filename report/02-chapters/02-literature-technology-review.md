## Literature Review

The literature review is a key part of your project with specific marking criteria. Its purpose is to show that you have **researched, understood, and critically evaluated** all aspects of your problem statement. This is your **"investigation"**.

### What to Include

- Describe the problem in detail.
- Mention any literature, organisations, institutions, or individuals important in the context or the solution.
- Correctly reference key articles, books, or papers using **IEEE style**.

### Critical Skills to Demonstrate

- **Summarise** the main points.
- **Discuss** (point out strengths, weaknesses, differences of approach, etc.).
- **Describe how the literature relates** to the problem you are addressing.
- **Apply your own critical thinking** to the material.
- **Draw your own conclusions** from your investigation and show how these will guide your methodology and implementation.

### Relevance

Everything in your literature review must be **clearly relevant** to your problem statement, aims, and objectives — and you must clearly state this relevance.

## Technology Review

The technology review focuses on technology that **will be** and **could be** used for the project.

### What to Include

1. **Review and summarise** different technology options you could apply to your project.
2. Provide the **rationale for your own choice**.

## Summary

Provide a **critical summary** of the section, including:

- The literature and technologies' **benefits** and **limitations**.
- How your critical analysis outcomes will **influence your methodology** and your project overall.
